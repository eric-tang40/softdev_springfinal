Data Analysis for Past 30 Weeks

(Fridays: November 17, 2023 to June 7th, 2024)

Here, we can see that most songs stay less than 10 weeks on the billboard, with over 100 songs. After that, there is an even distribution of around 30 songs for each cluster until just under 40 weeks. Then, there is another distribution of around 10 songs until just under 65 weeks.

Most songs' peak position is at number 1. Then, it seems to descend in numerical order, but peaks of 5 to 10 don't seem to have any correlation for the frequency of those peak positions.

Most explicit songs don't stay on the billboard for much time, with most having a tenure of under 10 weeks. However, the non-explicit songs have a more even distribution, with most songs having a tenure of greater than 20 weeks on the chart. This indicates that non-explicit songs experience greater success.

This ranking is just a bit of interesting data for the change in rankings on the billboard. The largest change is 54 spots, with the song Act II: Date @ 8. All the big changes are in the green, due to a large fall not physically being able to be shown on the billboard anymore.
